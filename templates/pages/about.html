MEDICS database contains searchable data on the academic studies and medical careers of all Finnish physicians who graduated and were entered into the medical register between 1896 and 1929. Their total number is a little short of 1000. Since the primary selection criterion has been graduation and “legitimation” (laillistaminen), or medical registration, the cohort does not include people who studied medicine but did not graduate as licentiates of medicine. Licentiate of Medicine degree was (and is) the basic medical degree in Finland. Neither does MEDICS include foreign doctors who worked in Finland with a special permission, “venia practicandi”. Almost all doctors included in the database graduated from the Medical Faculty of the University of Helsinki (called the Imperial Alexander University until 1917), which was the only medical school in the country, after which they were licensed by the National Board of Health (Lääkintöhallitus).

The data was collected from various public registers and publications (Lääkärien nimikirjat, lääkärimatrikkelit, Valtiokalenteri etc.) as part of the research project Exclusive Treatment: Women’s Entry into Medicine in Finland, 1882-1917, carried out by Senior Research Fellow Heini Hakosalo (University of Oulu) and funded by the Academy of Finland.

None: Dentists (i.e. licentiates of odontology) are not included in the database, unless they also graduated as licentiates of medicine.

The interface was designed and executed by Shukhrat Mirrakhimov, Maria Shushakova, Polina Chainikova and Daria Efimova and first published online on December 2021.
